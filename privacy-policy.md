# Privacy Policy — VoiceRecorder

*Last updated: May 2026*

---

## Who we are

VoiceRecorder is developed by Johanna OS, an individual developer based in Sweden. This app is a tool for Swedish folk musicians to record audio and attach metadata to their recordings.

## The short version

Your recordings and metadata never leave your device unless you choose to share them yourself. The only data sent anywhere is anonymous technical information used to detect app crashes.

---

## What data the app collects

### Data stored on your device only

The following is stored locally in the app's database and never transmitted anywhere:

- **Audio recordings** — files you record using the app, saved to your device's Music/VoiceRecorder folder
- **Recording metadata** — title, of/after, from, song type, performer name, notes, and any tags you create
- **App settings** — field configuration, tag colors, filter preferences

This data is entirely under your control. It is never uploaded, synced, or shared with anyone — including the developer — unless you explicitly use the Share function.

### Data sent to Sentry (crash reporting)

The app uses Sentry (sentry.io) to detect and report technical errors. When the app crashes or encounters an error, the following technical information is sent to Sentry's servers:

- **A random device identifier** — a randomly generated code created when you first install the app, stored only on your device. This is used to group errors from the same device together. It is not linked to your name, Google account, or any other personal identifier. It cannot be used to identify you.
- **App version and Android version**
- **Device model** (e.g. Samsung Galaxy A55)
- **A sequence of technical events leading up to the error** (e.g. "recording started", "file save succeeded") — these contain no user-entered content such as tune titles or performer names
- **Stack traces** — technical code information showing where an error occurred

Sentry does not receive:
- Your recordings or audio files
- Tune titles, performer names, notes, or any metadata you enter
- Your name, email, or any account information
- Your IP address
- Location data

Sentry is a US-based service. Data sent to Sentry is governed by Sentry's own privacy policy (sentry.io/privacy). As the developer, I can view crash reports in the Sentry dashboard to identify and fix bugs.

If you want to understand exactly what Sentry receives, you can view the open source code for this app at https://github.com/johannaOS/MetaDataRecorder.

---

## Microphone access

The app requests microphone permission in order to record audio. The microphone is only active when you have explicitly started a recording. Audio is saved directly to your device and is never transmitted.

---

## Storage access

The app requests storage permission to save recordings to your device's Music/VoiceRecorder folder and to import audio files you choose to import.

---

## Sharing

If you use the Share function on a recording, the audio file is shared using your device's standard sharing system (Android share sheet). The app has no control over what happens to the file after you share it — that depends on the app you share it to.

---

## Data retention

All data stored by the app remains on your device until you delete it. Deleting a recording removes both the database entry and the audio file (you are asked whether to also remove it from Music/VoiceRecorder). Uninstalling the app removes all app data from the device.

Crash reports sent to Sentry are retained according to Sentry's data retention policy (90 days on the free plan).

---

## Your rights under GDPR

You are located in the EU/EEA, so the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies.

Since almost all data stays on your device and is under your direct control, most GDPR rights are exercised simply by using the app's own delete functions.

For the limited technical data sent to Sentry:
- The random device identifier cannot be linked back to you without access to your physical device, making it effectively anonymous in practice
- You can request deletion of Sentry data by contacting the developer at johanna.o.s@gmail.com
